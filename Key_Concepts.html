  • Tags: Words that are enclosed with a less than sign and a greater than sign. They typically are used to indicate they type of content, place, and/or importance of the content and is a piece of an element.

  • Attributes: Provide extra information inside of an opening tag. This can include text, urls, images, and other similar things to enhance a webpage.

  • Elements: Elements refer to a combination of attributes, tags, and content which come together to create a complete piece of displayable content on a webpage

  • Block vs. Inline: This refers to how elements take up space in code. Block elements typically starts on their own line and consume a lot of space and Inline does not while consuming a comparatively smaller amount of space.

  • Structure: Structure typically refers to two things depending on the context. The first is the layout of the page and where content will show up. The other is the heirarchy and ordering of an HTML document with everything existing inside tags at different levels of the heirarchy.

  • Semantic Elements: HTML tags added to describe the content rather that the structure of the content. Generally used for improving accessibility for people with different impairments, but can also help with increasing a websites visibility in search engines.

  • Cascading Style Sheets(CSS): A language used to further define the look of a website and its presentation. It sets the bounds and a select number of rules to create cleaner more visually appealing webpages.
